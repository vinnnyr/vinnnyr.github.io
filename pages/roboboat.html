Georgia Tech Roboboat Team

As part of the Georgia Tech RoboBoat Team part of the Aerospace Systems and Design Laboratory (ASDL) at Georgia Tech, I have been leading the effort since 2017 to design build and test new iterations of our robotic, autonomous boat for the AUVSI/RoboNation Roboboat competition.
The main motivation behind the design of this vehicle is ensuring the team has a reliable hardware platform so that we can properly demonstrate the capabilities of the autonomy software.

While in the past we reused an older hardware platform, in 2017 I led the effort to build a brand new boat named "Rich Girl" .
Design considerations included:

  • maximizing size of the vehicle to have a large deck size for UAV landing
  • adding a larger capacity battery, allowing for minimization of two major time sinks:
    • charging the battery
    • moving the boat in and out of the water
  • improving speed and turning speed of the vehicle
  • modularity -- perhaps the most important aspect of all.
    • this ensures that our efforts result in a hardware platform that can be used for years to come, with minimal major hardware changes
    • The modularity of the vehicle allowed us to pursue a new, lighter pontoon design for the 2018 vehicle

You can view our 2017 Technical Design Report hosted on the RoboNation website. The team is currently working towards bringing a 2018 vehicle to competition.
